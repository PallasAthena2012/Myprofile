About Me

Hello, my name is Ilyannie!

My journey began with the publication of my first short story, Pumpkin by Ilyannie T.G., in light of the COVID-19 pandemic. Since then, my love for words has blossomed thanks to the newfound flexibility offered by my online school community, Washington Virtual Academies. From 2021 to 2023, I was named the three-time champion of the K12 National Spelling Bee. This passion eventually evolved into a profound interest in public speaking and debate, through which I received the Potomac Debate Tournament First Place Speaker Award twice in a row and represented Washington State at the 2024 National Civics Bee National Championship, harnessing it as a platform to raise awareness about the issue of gun violence.

Beyond humanities, however, I am also an environmental advocate, having placed first at the 2024 K12 National Innovation Challenge after presenting my project, “Operation BioTide”, a Caroline D. Bradley Scholarship Finalist, an active member of my school's Associated Student Body, where I have spearheaded several programs including a Journalism Team and Civics Club. I have also received International recognition as a competitive pianist, having been awarded Silver Medals at the Bach and Charleston International Festivals and the Distinguished Gold Medal at the Classical International Viennese Festival. In 2025, my musical career came to a climax when I placed first at the Crescendo International Competition and had the chance to perform my debut solo at Carnegie Hall’s Zankel Hall. Looking ahead, I was recently accepted into the UW Robinson Center for Young Scholars, empowering me with the opportunity of a lifetime to pursue a one-year transition from middle school directly into college.
